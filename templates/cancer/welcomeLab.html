Breast Cancer



There can be life after breast cancer, the prerequisite is early detection and not being afraid of it.


------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Symptoms

  • Breast Pain
  • Inverted Nipple
  • Bloddy discharge from nipple
  • Breast Lumps
  • Breast Swelling


Stages

Breast Cancer has 5 stages:

  • Stage 0 (DCIS)
  • Stage 1
    • Stage 1A :Lymph nodes are not affected
    • Stage 1B :Tumor found nearby Lymph nodes
  • Stage 2
    • Stage 2A :Tumor measures less than 2cms
    • Stage 2B :Tumor measures between 2cms and 5cms
  • Stage 3
    • Stage 3A :Tumor measures more than 5cms
    • Stage 3B :Tumor invades chest wall
    • Stage 3C :Tumor found in more than 10 lymph nodes
  • Stage 4 :Tumor has spread to distant lymph nodes and organs


Types

Breast Cancers fall under :

  • Invasive
  • Non-Invasive
These categories are used to describe the following types:
  • Ductal Carcinoma In Situ
  • Lobular Carcinoma In Situ
  • Invasive Ductal Carcinoma
  • Invasive Lobular Carcinoma


Risks

  • Getting older: The risk for breast cancer increases with age; most breast cancers are diagnosed after age 50.
  • Genetic mutations: Inherited changes (mutations) to certain genes, such as BRCA1 and BRCA2. Women who have inherited these genetic changes are at higher risk of breast and ovarian cancer.
  • Personal history of breast cancer or certain non-cancerous breast diseases: Women who have had breast cancer are more likely to get breast cancer a second time. Some non-cancerous breast diseases such as atypical hyperplasia or lobular carcinoma in situ are associated with a higher risk of getting breast cancer.
  • Family history of breast cancer: A woman’s risk for breast cancer is higher if she has a mother, sister, or daughter (first-degree relative) or multiple family members on either her mother’s or father’s side of the family who have had breast cancer.


Prostate Cancer



While you may not have chosen to have prostate cancer, you can choose how you want to be treated!


------------------------------------------------------------------------------


Symptoms

  • Painful urination
  • Bloody discharge in urine
  • Difficulty in maintaining erection
  • Frequent urge to urinate
  • Pain or discomfort while sitting


Stages

The stages of prostate cancer can inform the appropriate decisions for the rightful treatment.

  • Stage 0: Presence of pre-cancerous cells
  • Stage 1 (Localized): Cancer present only in the prostate gland
  • Stage 2-3 (Regional): Cancer spreads to nearby tissues
  • Stage 4 (Distant): Cancer can spread to other parts of the body


Types

  • Adenocarcinomas (Common)
  • Transitional cell carcinomas (Rare)
  • Small cell carcinomas (Rare)
  • Neuroendocrime Tumors (Rare)


Risks

  • Age: The risk increases after the age of 50. Rare before the age of 45.
  • Race (or) Ethinicity: It is more common in black males than white males.
  • Family History: If prostate cancer exists in the family history, one is more likely to have it in the further generations.
  • Genetic Factors: The presence of some inherant fetaures such as BRCA1 and BRCA2 increases the risk of prostate cancer.
